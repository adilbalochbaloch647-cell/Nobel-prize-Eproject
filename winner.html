Nobel Prize Winners

Albert Einstein

A.E received the Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of the photoelectric effect, which laid the foundation for modern quantum theory.

Marie Curie

Marie Curie was awarded the Nobel Prize for her pioneering research on radioactivity. She became the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Nelson Mandela

Nelson Mandela received the Nobel Peace Prize for his role in ending apartheid in South Africa. His lifelong commitment to peace, reconciliation, and equality inspired the world.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for her courageous fight for girls’ education. She became the youngest Nobel laureate in history.

Mother Teresa

Mother Teresa received the Nobel Peace Prize for her humanitarian work among the poor and sick. Her compassion and dedication improved countless lives worldwide.

Martin Luther King Jr.

M.L King Jr. was honored with the Nobel Peace Prize for his non-violent struggle racial discrimination. His leadership advanced civil rights and social justice.

Alexander Fleming

Alexander Fleming received the Nobel Prize in Medicine for discovering penicillin. His discovery transformed medical treatment and saved millions of lives.

Ernest Hemingway

Ernest Hemingway was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature for his powerful storytelling style. His works had a lasting impact on modern literature.

Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore received the Nobel Prize in Literature for his profound poetry and writings. He was the first non-European Nobel laureate in literature.

Marie Curie (Chemistry)

Marie Curie also won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for discovering two new elements, polonium and radium. Her scientific achievements advanced chemistry and medicine.

Amartya Sen

Amartya Sen was awarded the Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences for his contributions to welfare economics. His work focused on poverty and human development.

Abdul Sattar Edhi

Although not a Nobel laureate, Abdul Sattar Edhi is globally recognized for his humanitarian services. His life of selfless service is often regarded as Nobel-worthy.
